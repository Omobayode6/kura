Privacy Policy

Kura (“Kura”, “we”, “us”, “our”), recognizes your privacy rights as guaranteed under the 1999 Constitution of the Federal Republic of Nigeria: The Nigerian Data Protection Regulation, and other applicable privacy laws in Nigeria. Thus, it is important to us as a law-abiding organisation that your Personal Data is managed, processed, and protected in accordance with the provisions of the applicable laws. In the course of our business and/or your engagement with us and third parties through our Platforms (this includes but are not limited to our websites, digital platforms, mobile applications, physical operations/offices, amongst others), we may process your Personal Data, subject however to the terms of this Policy.

This Privacy Policy (“Policy”) therefore explains our privacy practices with respect to how we collect, process your Personal Data, and describes your rights as a user our services and Platform. In this Privacy Policy, “we”, “us” or “our” refers to Kura and its successors, agents and assigns, while “you”, ““your” refers to you and/or any person who subscribes for, uses or authorises the use of the Service.

As a user of any of our Platforms, you accept this Privacy Policy when you sign up for, or use our products, services, content, technologies, or functions offered on our Platforms and all related sites and services (“Services”).

Our Privacy Policy explains our user’s privacy rights regarding:

  • What information we collect.
  • Why we collect such information.
  • The use of such information.
  • The storage, sharing and protection of such information.

“Kura”, “we” or “us” means Kura.

Consent

What information do we collect?

Collection of Personal Data

In the course of your engagement with us or with third parties through our platforms, we may collect your Personal Data in the following ways:

Data you give us: We may collect and process your information when you open an account on our platform, and/or update your account on our platform. We may collect information such as your contact information, which includes your name, phone number, location, email and others. We may collect information such as your date of birth, school degree and course information, occupation, and social media account handles. We may also collect Information from data you provide to us through documents like Identification cards or through any other means by which you wilfully provide information to us.

Automatic Information collection: We may automatically collect Information upon your engagement with us or our Platforms through your computer, mobile phones, and other access devices. The information sent includes, User-ID known as account ID generated by us, to users who sign up on the platform, your geo-location information, geotagging, IP address, traffic to and from our platform.

Social Media: We also receive Information through your engagements with us on social media sites (e.g., Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, etc). This includes but is not limited to your replies to our posts, your comments, enquiries, messages to us, etc. We may also collect information from your public profile and updates on social media.

We also collect:

  • In app messaging content such as sender, recipient, and contents of the message.
  • Your email content sent to us for enquiries and complaints, Including subject line, sender’s email address, and contents of the email.
  • Your photos or videos when you make posts on our platform (user logs and activities).
  • Any other data collected to improve the app performance e.g., rate, statistics, surveys, and study data.

Accessing your Personal Information

When accessing our Platforms, we may collect information about you and your interactions with the Services to which we undertake to keep secure and confidential.


We may offer you the ability to connect with our Platforms using a mobile device, either through a mobile application (App), computer, mobile optimised website, or by any other means. The provisions of this Privacy Policy apply to all such mobile access and use of mobile devices.


When you download or use our mobile applications, or access one of our mobile optimised sites, we may receive information about your location and your mobile device, including a unique identifier for your device. Storage and protection of your Data

We protect your personal Information using physical, technical, and administrative security measures to reduce the risks of loss, misuse, unauthorised access, unauthorised disclosure, and alteration. Some of our safeguards include firewall protection, encryption of data, authorization keys for authorised access, other servers and user data encryption, and strong passwords. Access to our various platforms is restricted to authorised users only. Your Information is also stored on our secure servers as well as cloud infrastructure. Please be aware that, despite our best efforts, no security measures are perfect or impenetrable. We will retain your personal information for the length of time needed to fulfil the purposes outlined in this privacy policy unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law. In a case where you have deleted your account, we will retain your information for at least 3 months. To dispose of personal data, we may anonymize it, delete it or take other appropriate steps.


We will take all necessary measures to ensure that your personal data is safe, however, you are also required to ensure that passwords, usernames, and all other information or hints that may enable third party access to your accounts on our platforms are secure. We therefore strongly advise you to keep such information secure and confidential. If you use a third party’s device (laptops, phones, public internet, etc.) to access your account, kindly ensure that you always log out when finished. Kindly note however that certain devices are programmed to save passwords or usernames, as such, we advise that you use third party devices with extreme caution. If you believe that an unauthorised person has accessed your information, please reach us at immediately at contact@thekura.co.

In order to execute our obligations to you, we may be required to process your Information, such as your name, contact details, or other information needed. For completeness, in the course of your engagement with us or use of our services and platforms, we may share your information with different stakeholders, including but not limited to service providers; Regulatory or judicial authorities; or other third parties pursuant to a subpoena, court order, or other legal process or requirement applicable to Kura. Please note that the aforementioned parties may be within or outside Nigeria.

In all cases, we will ensure that your Information is properly and professionally managed and notify the receiving party of the confidential nature of your Information, particularly the need to maintain the confidentiality of same and prevent unlawful or unauthorised usage.

  • Right to access your personal information being held by us. Requests for such information may be sent to contact@thekura.co.

In any event, we will communicate the reason for our refusal).

  • Right to withdraw consent for the processing of your information, provided that such withdrawal shall not invalidate any processing hitherto done based on the consent previously given by you.
  • Right to request that your personal data be deleted. We may however continue to retain the information where required under law, contract, regulatory authorities, or court of law.
